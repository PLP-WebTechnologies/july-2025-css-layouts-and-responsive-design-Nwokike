How to Cook Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a rich and incredibly tasty one-pot dish. It's a festive staple, perfect for celebrations, family gatherings, or just a delicious weeknight dinner. This recipe will guide you through creating that authentic, smoky flavor everyone loves.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 4-5 fresh plum tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper (tatashe)
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 3 stock cubes (Maggi/Knorr)
  • 5 cups chicken or beef stock
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend Peppers: Blend the tomatoes, red bell pepper, and scotch bonnets until smooth to create your pepper base.
  2. Saut√© Onions: Heat oil in a large pot. Add chopped onions and fry until translucent.
  3. Cook Paste & Base: Stir in the tomato paste and fry for 5 minutes. Then, pour in the blended pepper base and cook for 15-20 minutes until the oil separates.
  4. Season: Add thyme, curry powder, and crushed stock cubes. Stir well.
  5. Cook the Rice: Add the washed rice to the pot and stir until it's well-coated with the sauce. Pour in the stock, add salt, and bring to a boil.
  6. Simmer: Cover the pot tightly with foil and a lid. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes until the rice is cooked through. Do not stir during this time!

Pro Tips

For that classic smoky "party jollof" flavor, let the rice burn *just a little* at the bottom of the pot in the final 10 minutes of cooking. Don't stir it in, but the aroma will infuse the dish.

Always use parboiled rice; it holds its structure better and doesn't get mushy.

Jollof Essentials

The key to great Jollof is patience. Let your pepper base fry properly to develop a deep, rich flavor before adding the rice.

  • The Base: A well-cooked tomato and pepper sauce is crucial.
  • The Rice: Long-grain parboiled rice is non-negotiable.
  • The Steam: Trapping the steam is what cooks the rice perfectly.
  • Serve with: Fried plantain, chicken, or a fresh salad.
